# Introduction to NLP (S26) - Project Details

### January 12, 2026

## 1 General Instructions

Please read and follow the instructions here and mentioned later thoroughly.

1. This is a group project with a team size of 3-4.
2. The project must be implemented in Python.
3. Project component weights are according to the course website.
4. Mentors, usually with experience in your project areas, will be assigned
    for projects once project proposals are accepted. You are highly en-
    couraged to reach out to your mentors for project related questions
    and guidance.
5. Please use the TA office hours prior to mentor assignment to get inputs
    on your project ideas.
6. You are allowed to use AI tools or any internet resources for imple-
    menting the project. You are not allowed to use AI to generate

7. Please start early and follow the schedule as no extensions will be

## 2 Projects

The course project is a significant component of this course (45%), providing
an opportunity to apply the concepts and techniques learned to a real-world
NLP problem. Students will work in teams of 3 or 4 to propose, develop,
and present a project. Projects are expected to be at an intermediate or


advanced level. Submission to conferences/workshops is highly encouraged.
Read more on research opportunities.

The projects will have four phases. Details on expectations at each stage are
provided.

1. Interim Project Proposal: A brief document outlining your project
    idea in a couple of paragraphs.
2. Project Proposal: A document outlining your project idea, back-
    ground, goals, dataset, and methodology in 2-3 pages.
3. Mid Submission: Mid checkin on your progress.
4. Final Submission: A comprehensive paper/report detailing your
    project, experiments, results, and conclusions along with code/other
    resources and presentation or demonstration of your project.

### Interim Project Proposal

Deadline: 24 January 2026, 23:

The submission will be a document outlining the project idea in 1-2 para-
graphs. This would be similar to the abstract of a paper. The interim
proposal will be used to approve or revise/reject the project idea. You are
highly encouraged to discuss your ideas with TAs before submitting the pro-
posal. Teams will get 2-3 days to revise proposals and get them approved
before the final proposal deadline.

Submit the interim proposal as PDF, in the format <TeamName>-Proposal.pdf.
One submission per team.

### Project Proposal

Deadline: 31 January 2026, 23:

The submission will be a document outlining the project idea, background/related
work of relevant papers, objective/motivation, dataset, proposed methodol-
ogy, and evaluation options in 2-3 pages along with references. The final
proposal is intended to fix the scope for your interim and final submissions.
You may include a tentative timeline.


The proposal must follow the ACL Style Template, which is available here.

Submit the final proposal as PDF, in the format <TeamName>-Outline.pdf.
One submission per team.

You will be graded on the report.

### Project Mid Submission

Deadline: 8 March 2026, 23:

Each team must submit a 8-10 page document (excluding references) that
includes the project idea and motivation/objectives, background and related
work (with references to relevant and updated papers), dataset details, pro-
posed methodology/architecture and evaluation options. Also include the
progress so far (objectives completed, ongoing, and planned) and tentative
timeline.

The purpose of this document is to provide a clear picture of your project’s
direction and current progress.

The report must follow the ACL Style Template, which is available here.

Additionally, please either - make your GitHub repository public and share
the link, or share access privately with your mentor and co-mentors.

Submit as a PDF with the filename: <TeamName>-Mid.pdf. One sub-
mission per team.

You will be graded on the code, report and a viva. Having slides for the viva
is optional.

### Project Final Submission

Deadline: 8 April 2026, 23:

For the final submission, you are expected to have completed the implemen-
tation along with sufficient analysis of results. This includes quantitative
analysis showing the performance with suitable metrics and qualitative anal-


ysis demonstrating the expected output for a few test cases. For projects
proposing new datasets, this includes a thorough dataset summary and base-
lines.

The final report is also expected in the ACL Template. The report is ex-
pected to contain an abstract, followed by the introduction of the project,
literature study, dataset details, methodology (including training setup and
experiment details), evaluation, analysis of results and finally a conclusion.
We recommend the analysis section to include details on positive and nega-
tive results and any ablations you may have conducted. You may also include
challenges, limitations, ethics and future work. Please limit the report to 9
pages(not including references)

Along with the report, also include a well-designed, well-illustrated presenta-
tion which would include all the points covered above. Avoid copying content
directly from the report for the presentation. The presentation will be used
for the project walkthrough during final evaluation.

The final submission should have the following files compressed in a .zip with
the name <TeamName>-Final.zip:

1. Code
2. Report (Also include a link to the GitHub, if public and links to models
    in drive or HuggingFace)
3. Presentation (Preferred as PDF)
